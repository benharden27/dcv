We’re going to put together what we’ve discussed so far with regards to useful mechanics in writing for vocal communication to evaluate an environmental story. This level will take the form of a small-group discussion.

Listening

The following are the radio pieces that we are going to discuss in class. Check Google Classroom for which one you are assigned to.

Piece 1

Story: A Single Fire Killed Thousands Of Sequoias. Scientists Are Racing To Save The Rest

Program: NPR Morning Edition

Reporter: Lauren Sommer

Piece 2

Story: Drought, frost takes a massive toll on coffee crops in Brazil

Program: The World

Reporter: Michael Fox

Discussion

You should listen to you assigned story a few times and take notes on the following questions:

  • Focusing on the narrator: What examples of good writing for voice did you hear?
  • What climate/environment/other complex concepts needed to be explain/discussed and how were these handled?
  • With regards to the reading in Level 2, what aspects of the piece did you find effective?
  • What changes could be made to improve the story?

Once we’ve had the discussion in class, you should fill out an exit ticket.
