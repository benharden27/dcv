Effective design for communication isn’t just limited to the figures you produce for a paper or infographic, it’s the sum of all the elements on your presentation - the figure, the text, the images, the titling. In this level we’re going to think about these other elements and how they are combined for one specific communication tool - the scientific poster.

Some of you may have already made a scientific poster - they are usually big displays (48’‘x36’’) of the results of a scientific experiment, fieldwork, methodology, etc. that scientists show at a conference. We’re going to be making one for our oceanographic research proposal.

I will assign you a group and a poster to discuss. You should review your poster together and think about the answers to the following:

  • What is the audience for this poster?
  • How easy was it to get the general message? Why?
  • What design elements were effective?
  • What design elements were ineffective?

In class you will present your ideas on these points and we will talk as a group more generally about good design strategies.
