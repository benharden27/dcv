ARGO Data

ARGO are a fleet of autonomous ocean floats that travel through the ocean at around 1000 m. Every week or so, most descend to 2000 m before taking an ocean profiles from that depth to the surface where they beam data back to a data hub by satellite. Most floats record typical CTD measurements (temperature and salinity), but some also have additional sensors.

A good source of this data is from the Euro Argo site which has a nice interface to select floats and download their data.

I’m including some formatted data downloaded from this site for one float 5905852. You can download this data here

The fields are:

  • platform_code: The unique ID of the ARGO float
  • dttm: Date and time
  • lon: Longitude (°E)
  • lat: Latitude (°N)
  • pres: Pressure (dbar)
  • psal: Practical Salinity
  • temp: Temperature (°C)

Like the CTD data from previous SEA Cruises, the profiles are stacked on top of each other in the data. Unique values of longitude and latitude indicate a new profile.
